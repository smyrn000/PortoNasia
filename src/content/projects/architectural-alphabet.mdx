## Reading the city as type

*Architectural Alphabet* is a study in looking. Walking the coast and the streets
with a camera, the project hunts for the entire alphabet already present in the
built and natural world — a letter in a bent railing, a perfect **O** in a
manhole cover, a **Y** where two branches fork against the sky.

Stripped to black and white, each photograph removes colour and context so that
**form** is all that remains. Light, shadow and silhouette do the work of the
pen. Collected together, the twenty-six finds become a single typographic
specimen sheet — proof that composition, framing and a designer's eye can turn
ordinary infrastructure into language.

The series sits alongside the interior work as its quieter companion: the same
attention to proportion, rhythm and detail, trained on the city instead of the
room. It is where the technical and the artistic meet — the habit of seeing that
underpins everything else in the studio.
